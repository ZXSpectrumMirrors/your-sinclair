Wow. Basically!
    Due to various work commitments, the site had to be put mainly on the back-burner, but it still had it's ground-breaking features. Macromedia Flash movies were the big thing, so I started playing about with it. First came the (basic) 30 second intro in March, and that was followed by a 4 minute mini-movie in November. Also a couple of simple Java applets made their appearance, and in July the site had a complete makeover from a fairly simple design to the rather fancier appearance you get today. Add to that a few new features, and the site's not done too badly as a result of my free time being somewhat shortened.
    Popularity-wise, the site's completely exceeded all expectations, almost tripling over the past twelve months, plus gaining two website awards. With Christmas and New Year out of the way, the site now gets roughly 400 visitors a day (from about 1,400 hits). There are quite a few things planned for the site this year, but we'll see what happens time-wise, but 2000 looks like being a better year than 1999.
    It's all going to be rather good!

THINGS TO NOTE:

All stats generated from web logs sent to me from ClaraNET. If ClaraNET doesn't send me the stats due to a server failure, or if I lose the stats (like when I lost about two week's worth in August 1998 (boring story)), then the stats are lower than they should have been. This is where the "Corrected" stats come in: these reports take the average stats for the days which do have stats and scales it up to a 31 day month. This allows me to see the growth (or otherwise) in interest in the site. An example would be trying to compare January and February stats - since February only has 28/9 days, its raw stats are probably going to be lower than January's, even though the site's been more popular.
    Stats which have been lost appear in the bar charts as either a ridiculously low number, or as "-----" in the quantity field.
