The early days of the Rock'n'Roll Years, when the site covered just the first 72 issues of YS, had no articles, a handful of screenshots and a smattering of graphics around the site. The incompleteness of the access logs is due to ClaraNET's first faltering steps in providing them.
    The YS Rock'n'Roll Years started out as a series of weekly Usenet posts providing summaries of Your Sinclair magazines - a kind of "In This Month In History" - which would complement the comp.sys.sinclair FAQ as a guide to which Spectrum games were hot and which were turkies. People started asking for the whole lot in one go, rather than six issues a month, so I started the website on 16th August 1997. Not a lot of people knew about it, though, and add the fact that there was actually very little on the site it only attracted a few visitors. About 20 a day in fact, generating 90 page impressions.
    Well. Everyone has to start somewhere...

THINGS TO NOTE:

All stats generated from web logs sent to me from ClaraNET. If ClaraNET doesn't send me the stats due to a server failure, or if I lose the stats (like when I lost about two week's worth in August 1998 (boring story)), then the stats are lower than they should have been. This is where the "Corrected" stats come in: these reports take the average stats for the days which do have stats and scales it up to a 31 day month. This allows me to see the growth (or otherwise) in interest in the site. An example would be trying to compare January and February stats - since February only has 28/9 days, its raw stats are probably going to be lower than January's, even though the site's been more popular.
    Stats which have been lost appear in the bar charts as either a ridiculously low number, or as "-----" in the quantity field.
