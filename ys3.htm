Your Computer

March 1986
Issue 3

Games for the new Spectrum 128 are announced.
    Sinclair is rumoured to have three new machines coming soon: Speccy 128, QL Plus (CP/M compatible, built-in disk drives), and the Sinclair Portable (flat screen, 3.5" disk drive, CP/M and Spectrum compatible).
    Iolo Davidson expresses doubts about the Speccy 128's backwards compatibility.
    US Gold takes control of manufacturing, promotion, marketing and sales of all Ultimate products. According to US Gold, it's the "ultimate new year resolution". Groan...
    Porn comes to the Speccy with the release of Posthorn by the company of the same name. Priced at £15.50 it's said to combine the tension of Strip Poker and the fun of Postman's Knock. The mind boggles, especially as it claims to not have any sound or graphics...
    CRL announces that it's bringing out a game based on a new film, but won't say anything about it apart from that it'll be "definitely different".
