14th December 2004 : The 1984 chapter of the YSRnRY TV Show is OUT NOW! Featuring the rise and rise of the games industry, the crash of Imagine, plus many other things, this 7min 20sec MPEG isn't to be missed!
23rd October 2004 : The new issue of RetroGamer (which anyone in the world can buy online) should be out on the streets either now or in the next few days. If you're still unsure whether or not to buy it, I've put up a review of YS94 - well, more of an analysis! - but the long and the short of it is that I think it's unmissable.
18th October 2004 : You lucky, lucky people! Issue 9 of RetroGamer comes with a... with a... I'm too excited to say! So here's what they say:
    UK and subscriber copies of issue nine will include a brand new issue of Your Sinclair magazine! This 32-page tribute resurrects many classic features including Pssst, YS Tipshop, Program Pitstop and Future Shocks. Former Your Sinclair writers also get involved, revealing what they've been up to in the intervening years. After a decade, YS is back and still number one for Spectrum games!

11th July 2004 : The next chapter of the YSRnRY TV Show is now available! This one is 8.5 minutes long and covers 1983. This was the year when the Speccy scene boomed and anything was attempted in order to make an honest buck. Featuring lots of game footage and a kicking 80's soundtrack, it's well worth a look!

23rd April 2004 : More tinkering, plus a link added to ZX Format magazine, a free downloadable magazine that's been going for a couple of years now (yes, yet again I'm slow with the links...). Anyway, the current issue has an interview with me about the TV Show project, plus it covers everything that's happened in the Speccy scene (emulated or otherwise). It's a good read!
19th April 2004 : A few twiddles here and there, the most important one being to update the link to Your Spectrum archive YRUA? - I've been meaning to get around to doing that for many months now. Sorry Jim! I've also added a plug below for the new Retro Gamer magazine. It's been going for three issues now and is a very good read indeed and looks like it's here to stay. With the editor being a huge Spectrum fan, you're more or less guaranteed top-notch Sinclair coverage, and the mag doesn't let you down. Currently available every six weeks, although I bet you it goes monthly very soon. Highly recommended.
