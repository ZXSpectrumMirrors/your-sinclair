Site history - 1997

1997 1998 1999 2000 2001 2002 2003 2004 2005 2006 2007 2008 2009 2010 2011 2012 2013

9th November 1997 : More screenshots and page backgrounds added. Text formatter tweaked to take into account multiple previews linked to screenshots.
4th October 1997 : October/November 1986 entries are in and up! Many thanks to Jim Grimwood for these.
2nd October 1997 : Hmmm... the problem with MSIE was a combination of a bug in my text formatter and Microsoft's inventive implementation of tables... MS appear to have cleared up their act with Explorer 4, so I've tidied up the pages as best as I could be bothered, bearing in mind that everyone who uses MSIE 3 will now be upgrading. It ain't perfect, but it'll do. Minimum spec for these pages is now MSIE 4.0 or Netscape 3, preferably with more than 256 colours.
16th September 1997 : ARSE!!!! Discovered that the web pages look awful when viewed by MS Explorer. This is due to my liberal use of close tags - Netscape ignores superfluous close tags, Explorer merely goes loopy. Will fix this soon. I've also started to add more Speccy-like backgrounds to the issue pages - let me know what you think of them. Some of them are admittedly a bit ropey. The general policy is that the background is generated from a game featured in the mag. If you have any good alternatives to some of the ropey ones, Email me!
5th September 1997 : Minor corrections to the text formatter. If you see anything odd about page layout, can you tell me? Ta.
4th September 1997 : Text formatter completed! Got rid of the awful preformatted text and learnt how to use tables. Also started adding links to screenshots - more will be added in the next update.
1st September 1997 : Minor corrections to the text formatter. Pages made a bit more colourful - Time to work on the graphics...
31st August 1997 : Text formatted better - now the pages are ready for graphics and to be made more colourful.
16th August 1997 : Page put up on the web in it's basic text form. Graphics will be added soon.
1st August 1997 : The very first YSRnRY posting sent to comp.sys.sinclair - and the seeds of a new website are sown...
