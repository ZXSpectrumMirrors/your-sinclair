WELCOME!
Every owner of the Sinclair ZX Spectrum, in any of its forms, should remember one of the most entertaining computer magazines ever published: Your Sinclair. YS ran as Your Spectrum for a couple of years before being relaunched as Your Sinclair at Christmas 1985.
    YS ran until 1993 and outlasted all the other Spectrum magazines that were sold at the time. During this time various breakthroughs in game design arose that we now all take for granted in these days of multi-megabyte memory, multicolour graphics home computer systems.
    The YS Rock'n'Roll Years aims to relive those days, so sit back, relax, and indulge yourself into some serious nostalgia.

WHAT'S NEW
TV SHOW PAGE LAST UPDATED: 18th September 2003

7th November 2003 : The Head Over Heels remake might be RetroSpec's best yet, but the first one they did was way back in 1999. A remake of Skool Daze (but brought up to date a bit) was written for DOS and called Klass Of '99. This remake put RetroSpec well and truly on the map and now, four years later, a Windows version has just come out which is well worth downloading. There's also a version available for the Gameboy Advance(!).

28th September 2003 : It's at times like these that really reinforces what a good bunch of people there are working in the Speccy emuscene. RetroSpec has just released a remake of Head Over Heels!

    Coded by Tomaz Kac, with music by Dorian Black and graphics by Graham Goring, this retro remake is wonderful, and compares very well with the original Spectrum release. The map is the same, the rooms are almost totally the same, as is the gameplay. Graham's new graphics really brings the game up to date, and the 3D graphics engine, complete with light sources and shadows, is really well put to use. I know there has been an attempt at a remake before, but it really didn't come off that well. The RetroSpec version, however, is virtually flawless.
    Head Over Heels is my favourite Spectrum game of all time, and I would have been disappointed if any remake was less than perfect. I give the RetroSpec version my full recommendation. Go on, download a copy! Did I mention it was completely free?
7th September 2003 : The site's a bit in stasis right now, however work on the TV Show has just had a boost.
