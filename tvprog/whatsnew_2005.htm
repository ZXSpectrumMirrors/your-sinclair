04/12/05: It's done! Again, the soundtrack was something I had to listen to a few times before I was completely happy with it, but overall I'm pretty happy with it. A year in the making... blimey...
     Anyway, I'm now just sorting out the hosting side of things, and I also want to revamp the downloads page tomorrow night. So... maybe it'll be available Monday night sometime.

01/12/05: I'm determined to get the 1985 chapter out this month. So, I've tweaked a couple of the video segments, tomorrow I'm going to tweak a few other things, then I'll mix in a soundtrack over this weekend.
    So... next week, then.

30/10/05: Well, it's the end of October, so why isn't it out?
    A number of Real Life things got in the way - they seem to becoming more frequent these days... sigh... - but I've made a major decision. I've scrapped the original 3D intro animation that I've been planning.
    That perked me up immensely. It's still going to be inspired by Elite, but the one I'm now developing isn't so fiddly and doesn't require so much manual tweaking. In fact in a very short amount of time I've managed to finalise the movements of all the objects. I now need to perfect the camera angles, twiddle about with a couple of objects themselves, add lazers and that should be it.
    As for the actual content, there were a few comments by my previewers, so I'm going to have to re-jig a couple of sections, and I've also put in an additional section which should come as a nice surprise, and certainly one that I enjoyed doing highly.
    So, the new release date? I'd like to say November, but the Real Life Stuff is stepping up a few gears, so might not be until December, which would be nice as the release of the 1985 chapter would come out not just 20 years after 1985, but also 20 years after the release of the first issue of Your Sinclair.
    Which is nice.

19/08/05: Tip for the day for all you budding animators out there: never do an animation that involves a lot of hand-crafting. This first full-size (well, 20-odd seconds) 3D animation which I'm going to use for the 1985 intro is, well, pretty hand-crafted, and as a result takes bloody ages to accompolish very little. After this one I'll stick to more-or-less 100% computer-programmed animations - far, farrr simpler, and also a lot more fun. Well, I'm the sort of person who likes emulating the 3D Ant Attack engine in Perl, so your own mileage may vary...
    Anyway...

23/07/05: Heh, I knew I shouldn't have set a deadline for myself :) Spent most of my recent time off work sleeping and cycling... 23/06/05: One of the benefits of being too hot to eat and sleep is that you get to do lots of coding on lots of raw energy. The 3D animation package is almost done - or rather the bits that I actually plan to use. Tonight I sorted out the parameterisation, and I have algorithms in my head for natural tilting and also chase cameras. Should be completed over this coming weekend.
    So... soon after that I'll do the 1985 intro animation, and then the chapter itself. Seeing as I have a whole lot of free time right now, with more coming my way soon, this could be out by the end of July...
    Stay tuned...

20/06/05: Fannnnn-tastic!

    OK, so far I can place and move the camera at any point, pointing it at any point, place and move an object at any point, and have that object point towards its path so that it looks like it's travelling along it. Can also add and move light-sources too. A demo is available for download - a short 21sec 3.6Mb MPEG file.
    And next... proper parameterisation of rotates, stretches, etc. A proper chase-camera option. Automatic tilting into curves. Generic parameterisation.
    It's all so do-able, it's scary...

16/06/05: The 3D animation package is turning out quite nicely, although development time has been a little sluggish due to Real Life Stuff. I'm still working on the first task: specifying light sources and camera positions. Currently have code to place and move lights and cameras anywhere, move them along a line, that sort of thing. The hardest part was finding out how to move in a circle in three dimensions, but I've cracked that and am now about to implement splines (moving in curved paths in various ways).

    Rendering time is OK, although the final renderings with full scenerey, action, reflections, lighting, etc will probably be painful...

    I found POV-RAY - a freeware raytracer that's been going for ten years, and available on many platforms. Lookie:
