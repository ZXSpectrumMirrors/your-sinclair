Computer Gamer

May 1986
Issue 5

Wafadrive special offer: £49.95, with 16k wafers @ £2.49 each, 64k @ £2.79, 128k @ £2.99...
    Interview with Nigel Alderton and Karen Trueman of Ghosts And Goblins, Commando and Chuckie Egg fame.
    Mike Gerrard starts his Adventure column.
    After the traditional last-minute delays, Sinclair Research finally gets around to releasing the Spectrum 128k. It then announced that the Pandora Portable will be released by the end of the year...
    Meanwhile, the Sinclair C5 is being sold in America as a leisure vehicle. Adverts have the headline "At 15 miles per hour, the only sound you can hear is the beating of your own happy heart".
    Elite announces it's got hold of the licence for the classic game Paperboy.
    Nigel Planer's agents complained rather loudly about Issue 2's cover depicting The Young Ones...
