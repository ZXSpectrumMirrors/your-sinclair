30th December 2001 : Finally got around to catching up with the work I'm meant to be doing on here.
    YS Adventures added up to YS60. All hints and tips to appear in The Tipshop soon.
    A Rage Hard article about Ex-Basic and the HATE preview have been added courtesy of Alan Maxwell.
    Now that screen resolutions are getting higher and higher, the 256x192 pixel screenshots are looking smaller and smaller. Now you can magnify them just by clicking on them.
    HKM screenshot added courtesy of John Coyne.
18th December 2001 : 500,000 UP!! We've topped the half-million visit mark! Still no site update - hopefully will do one over the holiday period.
12th August 2001 : Not alot happening, but expect a small site update "soon" as I'm currently scanning all the YS Adventure columns for The Tipshop, and this site will get the full articles. I might as well mention that a few minutes ago we got our 400,000th visitor, and that this site celebrates its fourth birthday on Thursday the 16th. Nothing is planned for it this time around, although feel free to play on the Swedish Speaking Sinclair Spectrum. I've also updated the About the webmaster page with a new piccy and updated the details slightly.
    Future plans for this site? Not sure, but after I've done the scanning for YS Adventures, I want to break off further OCR work to have a bit of fun. I want to finish off YS HyperCapers (very old alpha here), and then play around with VRML - want to do something based on either Head Over Heels or Knightmare. Plus there are various other programs I want to write, including Java versions of the Dicing With Death and Dogfight YS board games. We'll see - the fact that I've been wanting to do these for a few years already should tell you alot about the sort of timescales involved for completion :-)
    To be honest, anything can happen once I've done all I want to do for The Tipshop. Once I've had a holiday and a bit of a ponder, you'll no doubt see some "interesting" results on here. I think that the website has more than enough articles on it to be going on with (I think it's still the largest archive of its type on the web), so will diversify what this website offers. I'm also getting a few ideas for the next Flash movie, so that's something to look forward to. I haven't done a major one since the "Bond" one in 1999, although I rather liked the Xmas card I did last Christmas.
    Anyway, this is turning into a bit of a ramble, so I'll sign off now. Hopefully you'll get an idea of where I want to take this site from this History entry. Things will be fun, I'm just sick of scanning articles! :-)
1st July 2001 : More advert and inlay scans added.
6th June 2001 : All's been quiet on this website, but there's a good reason for it - I've been working on another! I've got together with Gerard Sweeney and, backed by a high-calibre team, have put together The Tipshop! We're aiming to be the central repository for all game hints, tips, maps, solutions, hacks, POKEs.... you name it, we want it in there. You'll notice that the site is now peppered with Tipshop icons - click on these to search the Tipshop database for any help for any given game, just like how the WOS buttons search WOS for a particular game. I hope you like it - it now covers over 2,000 games, with a total of nearly 4,500 entries, and we're only just starting!.

2nd April 2001 : Letters for issues 25-48 (1988/9) added.
31st March 2001 : Scans and cartoons handled a little differently. You can now view a thumbnail of a scan before downloading the full-sized version. Relevant Doodlebugs and Next Months added into the Letters pages.
17th March 2001 : Letters pages added for issues 1 to 24. Not too sure about the layout of the pages yet (any suggestions appreciated), but it's still a work-in-progress with more Letters to come.
    Stuart Brady has provided a better screenshot for SAM Astroball.
10th February 2001 : In moving towards the unofficial aim of this site by becoming a pretty front-end to the World Of Spectrum archive, I've added thumbnails of game adverts and inlays to each of the relevant articles. It really is incredible the feeling you get seeing the old adverts again, especially big names such as OutRun where the images were so strong. Whilst I'm here, I'll also say that doing something like this makes you realise the vastness of the whole World Of Spectrum project, and also how far it has gone already - I found the relevant scans for almost every single game that has a review on this site, including some quite obscure ones. Martijn van der Heide has done a lot for the Speccy scene by setting up and running this archive, and deserves all the praise he gets.
16th January 2001 : The relevant Spectrum screenshot has now been added to each arcade screenshot page.
14th January 2001 : A shedload of arcade screenshots added - now hope to cover every single game that was converted to the Speccy and is emulated by the MAME arcade emulator, so expect everything from Space Invaders to Street Fighter II to be included. Two more articles have been added - reviews for Motos and The Fast and the Furious have been typed up by Jamie Percival - many thanks to him for doing this for me!
1st January 2001 : HAPPY NEW YEAR!!! Plus, in the next couple of days, the web counter (which actually hand-counts visitors who are using web browsers rather than automated processes) is about to hit the milestone figure of 250,000!
