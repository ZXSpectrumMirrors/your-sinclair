Jon Pillar
Forget the games - on the title alone, the compilation has won me over. Ah well, suppose I'd better string out the suspense and get on with the reviews. (But I can tell you now - it's a winner.)
    
The Double
Let's start with a management game. The Double is a classic of the genre, with a thick fold-out inlay covered in tiny writing and screensful of well-presented lists. To the layperson it may appear exactly the same as every other management sim, but it's not. Probably. 70°
    
Soccer Challenge
Pleasantly different from the usual soccer game, Challenge has you brushing up on your footy skills in preparation for the competition course at the end. With passing, dribbling, tackling and penalties included there's plenty of variation in the gameplay. As a bonus the graphics are clean and detailed, and all in all it's quite spiffy. 78°
    
Rugby Boss
Numbers time! This management sim is from the programmer of the ancient Superbowl and is in much the same style. Again, if you like such sims you'll probably go a bundle on it. 70°
    
Australian Rules Football
Playing fair is not an option in this Kick Off-like game. Rising above the obligatory horrid graphics, the gameplay is an enjoyable twist on standard footy - there's lots of bashing into people rather than skilfully avoiding them. Manic fun. 80°
    Good stuff, with plenty to do whether you want to stretch your brain or flex your muscles. Great value, with an extra mark for that title.
