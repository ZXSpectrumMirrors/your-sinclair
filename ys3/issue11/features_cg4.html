As I mentioned earlier, the twist added by blending today with yesteryear's medium makes these diversions fascinating and entertaining. Most of us played these games many years ago and in some cases enjoyed them. I remember classrooms of kids queuing to play Blitz (or Bomber or whatever) on the Vic 20. And... sniff... and some kind soul entered it in 1997. How can someone class this immortal tasty dish as crap??? Sob.

In the 1999 crop the level of crapness reached new heights in Dopey's Castle, a text adventure with a deaf hero... you have to type every command twice. One other game looked like a commercial release from the early 1980's. Little Batty had excellent sprites and sound-effects and was genuinely crap: you can fly above the maze that the baddies move around in.

I could ramble through the hundreds of entries and pick out the games that are destined to be legends for you, but I don't want to spoil your fun. One thing is for sure, crap games are excellent.
