4. Look forward to reader feedback

Because you will never, ever, ever get any. YS3's been running since the summer of '99, and in that time it's received approximately fifty e-mails for the letters page. (And we're guessing, really, because we can't remember which ones Jon made up. Oh what a giveaway.) Of the ten or so halfway-coherent people who have written to us, five of them ended up writing features and things for YS3 themselves. Most of the people who write to us are either incapable of stringing together enough words to make a sentence, or can string together enough words, but in no particular order, or are just completely blimmin' hatstand. (Andy Kavanagh, for example.) We love all our readers, obv.; it'd just be nice if they wrote to us every now and then to let us know they exist. (Since we've taken away our hit counter, what with it being completely useless, we've no idea whatsoever how many people read YS3.)

Another thing you'll never, ever get is a female fan pledging their undying love to you. (Which, to be frank, is the only reason Jon and Nathan started this thing in the first place. Well, that and the hope of bribery with pastry produce.)
