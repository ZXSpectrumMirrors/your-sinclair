3. Review games

It seems like such an obvious thing to do. After all, what's the point of a computer if it's not to play games? (You know that's the real reason they were invented, after all. Cracking German codes and designing nuclear weapons? Playing computerised Battleships, more like.) And what better way to point people at the many, many freeware remakes that are around than to tell people how wonderful they are?

The trouble is, nobody likes writing reviews. (Except Jon.) Everybody spends so much time writing increasingly amusing and interesting features (like Pssst!, for example) that they've got no energy or motivation left to write a review properly. After all, it can take several hours of playing a game to really get a good feel for it, even if it's something as simple as a Jumping Jack or Defender remake, and when you've already spent a week making a bunch of sprites walk around the Spectrum screen, or single-handedly written Pssst!, it's unlikely that'll you still care enough to do anything about the complete lack of reviews. So the issue gets delayed for another month while you wait for someone to review a game. (And unless you have a Jon Hyde handy, it's probably going to be your editor doing it, twenty minutes before uploading the issue to the server, and getting all the screenshots wrong.)
