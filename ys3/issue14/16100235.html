5. Fall off the net

The trouble with the internet is that it costs money to get connected to it, and to stay on it. Nathan is notoriously bad at paying his phone bill. While this was fine up until around the beginning of 2001 (because his phone company lost his records and stopped charging him), he now has to pay actual money every month to stay online. Generally his variable employment status renders him incapable of doing this, especially when you take into account how much time he tends to spend online. (BT have given up sending normal bills and just send him a solicitor's letter every month.) Inevitably, this means that Nathan is offline just when an issue is more or less finished, or when somebody needs to talk to him to find out what they're supposed to be doing for the next issue.

Technically, this could have been absorbed into point one, but it's worth underlining the point - if you're making a webzine, don't let Nathan edit it. He's crap. (Oi! Oh, wait, you're right. Ed)
