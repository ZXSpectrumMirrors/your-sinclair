In the eleven months since the last issue, you'd think we'd have received some letters, wouldn't you? Well, we did, and, as is traditional, they were accidentally deleted. (It wasn't my fault, for a change. Honest.) It didn't make that much difference, though, since of the 660 e-mails received, only six (no, really, six) were actually meant for YS3, the rest being the usual insincere offers of free money, sex and cable TV. So, given that there's still this whole page dedicated to reader feedback to fill, I'm going to take advantage of this little opportunity to tell you what a useless bunch of freeloading basts most of you are.

A little harsh? I think not. When an issue of YS3 is late (as they so often are) there's no shortage of people to comment on it. But how many people are prepared to say 'Hurrah!' when an issue is released, let alone send a brief e-mail expressing some gratitude? Personally I'm not overly bothered; there's always a little (very little) congratulation from (very few) people on the comp.sys.sinclair newsgroup. But most of the YS3 team don't read the newsgroup; they rely on the letters we get from our readers for encouragement. (And me, but we've already established my crapness, and this page is about yours.) And what do we get? Very close to bugger all.

Am I really asking for that much? Just an e-mail to tell us what you think, what bits you like, what bits you dislike. Random nonsense about your life, if you want. Do you have any idea of the sacrifices we've made to bring each and every issue of YS3 to you? Do you know how many arguments with my girlfriend revolved around this magazine? Do you know I actually wrote articles for YS3 while I was in hospital? All we wanted was a bit of recognition, a bit of acknowledgement. But you people just refuse to e-mail us and carry on reading the free mag. You useless, cretinous morons.

And don't get me started on you people not telling your friends to read us, because I could go on all day.
