But, of course, The X Files isn't on at the moment, so we're left with their spiritual ancestors, Scooby and Shaggy, in this entertaining little point'n'click adventure. It's a surprising choice of game, given that every other Scooby Doo game has been a crappy platformer, and especially so when you take into account that this is both a Megadrive game and published by Acclaim, world-reknowned purveyors of shoddy cash-ins. It's at about this point, of course, that I hear you all cry 'Why are you reviewing a Megadrive game? Don't you know this is a Spectrum magazine?', to which, of course, there is only one proper response, which is that this is my Spectrum magazine, I'm the one spending far too much of my time writing stuff for you lot, and I'll review whatever the heck I want. (More polite would be 'I'll review any game that I think merits the attention, regardless of format, because it's not about the format, it's about the games.' But when have I ever gone for the polite option, eh?)

So. The game, then. As I said, it's a point'n'click adventure, which seems rather an obvious way to make a Scooby game when you think about it. Each of the two selectable adventures opens with Scoob and the gang in the Mystery Machine, setting the scene in TV show style. Moments into the game proper and the gang's split up, leaving the player in control of Shaggy, with Scooby offering his usual brand of help.
