If there's somebody out there who's done something wonderful, why not let them know how much you admire them for it? Go on, it won't hurt. If more people congratulated each other for doing good things, then a lot more good things'd get done, and that's to everybody's benefit.

Even better, why not let them know through the pages of this very magazine? If there's somebody you want to tell a global audience about, write and let us know all about them and what they've done that's so wonderful, and we'll tell the world. Spread the love!

Of the many fields of battle faced by man over the ages, few are as harsh and unforgiving as the comp.sys.sinclair newsgroup. True flaming is rare in this bastion of the instantly off-topic thread, given the incredibly few rules that anyone can agree to maintain. Perhaps once in a generation can anyone be agreed upon by more than two people as annoying, let alone worth flaming. And yet I have borne witness to it with my own eyes. WITH MY OWN EYES, I TELL YOU.

It must stop, for he is the Chosen Prophet of I, Nathan, King of Plugs.

There aren't many readers who bother to make themselves known to YS3. In fact, over the course of YS3's run of more than three years, perhaps fifty people have made the effort to write to us. Fewer make the effort to write about us, to let the world know about something they like. (We even tried bribery, but nobody was interested.) So imagine our surprise when somebody tried to spread the word using not only the power of art, but the power of the running joke.

It all started, of course, with Alex Farlie, and his alter ego, FMN8. In a post of random genius rivalling that of Coats of Fury, he posted the first Not Quite A Scrolly demo post. A collection of entirely arbitrary thoughts, typed in a stream-of-consciousness style worthy of the finest European demo coder, NQAS remains to this day perhaps the most utterly bizarre post ever made to any newsgroup.

Alex's contributions did not begin with NQAS. He sent YS3 a series of e-mails, each containing at least one absolutely brilliant idea for the magazine, none of which we've got around to doing yet. But our general uselessness has never dulled his love of YS3, and for that we are grateful. If only all our readers saw our path as clearly as him, the world would be a far more interesting place.

NQAS 4EVA
There have actually been at least twelve NQAS posts. Unfortunately the Prophet hasn't seen fit, for some reason, to e-mail them directly to us at YS3, which is rather annoying to say the least. Everyone who's a little bit unhinged should be sending us their random thoughts - we could do with the material.

How many of us could honestly say that we could come up with anything as inspired as NQAS? And how many of us would follow the emulation of European scrolly demo coders to its bitterly logical conclusion by writing similarly deranged and self-indulgent messages to the world not once, not twice, but simply over and over again until literally everybody loses track of the numbers, rendering it entirely pointless for me to even try to research this article? (Ahem.) And yet, in every single NQAS, without fail, there is always the dedicated half-complaint and half-prayer of the true believer - 'Where's the next issue of YS3?'

Alex, we salute you. Throughout your variably-received time as the Chosen Prophet, your enthusiasm has never waned, and your faith to the One True Magazine has been steadfast. Your place in heaven is assured, provided we can trade bytes posted for Holy Brownie Points.

Alex Farlie is the Chosen Prophet. IT IS MY WILL, and MY WILL... erm, WILL BE DONE.
