6. Hire a penguin

As if the general uselessness of our editor wasn't enough, we let him bring along his penguin. Taking over from Jon as Deputy Editor, Bob was supposed to be the ultimate in deadline enforcement. Unfortunately, after a couple of successful issues the bright lights of Hollywood beckoned to the young penguin, and he moved on to star in the sleeper hit Coats of Fury and the now legendary (well, nearly) Lethal Penguin, in which the rift between Bob and the man he replaced on YS3 was healed in a buddy-action-comedy flick. By now you've probably also seen the sequel, Lethal Penguin 2 - Lethaller Penguin, in which the two are reunited for a bigger, better, more expensive, more violent and more funny episode in the ongoing story of Detective Sergeants Jon Murtaugh and Bob Riggs. (Watch out for Lethal Penguin 3 - Lethallest Penguin later in the year. Yes, this year.)

Wonderful as Bob's film career is, though, it inevitably led to problems on YS3. Not only was the rift caused by his replacing Jon damaging to the magazine, but when he took Jon with him to Hollywood it left a fairly large gap in the magazine that could only be filled by the Herculean efforts of Phil Robertshaw, the only person to ever write more than Jon. (In fact, barring The Intermittently Libellous Detectives, Phil writes Pssst! by himself. And has done for the last five issues or so. A hearty round of applause and some fan mail are well-deserved, but of course the only mail we get is written by Jon, so he'll only be getting the applause. Oh what a giveaway.)
