Well I Never Expected That

Surprise followed by adulation greeted Tim Follin as he delurked on comp.sys.sinclair. A discussion on how his multi-channel music in games such as Agent X and Chronos brought the man himself out of hiding. He was quickly introduced to Retrospec, and asked if he could join! Does this mean that future Retrospec releases will be blessed with Mr Follin's talents? We can only hope.

Island Games

Sinclair fanzine Desert Island Disks has started a competition that promises to sort the men from the boys. Spectrum Tour 2000 will run for six months, starting from May 1st (that means it ends at the end of October, for the calendarless amongst you). Competitors are challenged to get the highest scores they can in ten classic games, including Chase HQ and Barbarian. Nathan's joined already, but don't expect much of a contest from him - he's already being quite thoroughly hammered. Not that we expected much of a showing from him. (Oi! - Ed.)

No It's Not, Damn You!

Reports on c.s.s. indicate that the long-running argument over the difficulty of Robocop have apparently been ended by the game's author, Mike Lamb. In a conversation with Graham Bosworth, the esteemed Mr Lamb is reported to have agreed that Robocop was 'quite hard'. This does of course mean that everyone here at the YS3 office is a superhuman gamesplayer with the skill and coordination of Jackie Chan.

Ay, Listen To This

C64 fans have long had the opportunity to download thousands of tunes made for the SID chip, from games and demos from everyone's favourite crap, unusably bad American computer. Now it's the turn of all us Spec- chums, with the launch of Project AY. Browse through and download some of the greatest soundtracks ever to see the Spectrum's sound chip. We're looking forward to another opportunity to burn some space on our hard- drives...

I've Started So I'll Finish

It's definitely competition time around here, with another new Spectrum-related site, this time with a Flash-based, Speccy-related quiz! Answer twenty questions on the Zedex82 site for the opportunity to see your name in lights (well, on the high-score table, anyway).
