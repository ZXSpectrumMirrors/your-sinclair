YS3 Megapreview!
I remember the first time I played The Pyramid. It was on the SoftAid tape, along with 3D Tank Duel and Ant Attack. I spent most of my time playing Ant Attack (I'll avoid the obvious plug), but once I'd finished that I turned my attention to the rest of the games on the tape. Then I found The Pyramid, and hours were wasted trying to fight my way through the fifteen levels.

And now, thanks to some hard work by Ric Haines, we can fight our way through a new pyramid. The game plays much the same; kill a number of bad guys in each chamber to collect crystals which unlock the door to the next chamber. The biggest difference is in the graphics. Written for DirectX, The Pyramid's sprites are far bigger, brighter and bolder than the original. And with the sound cranked up, it's an enjoyable blast through the first few levels that have been completed. It looks and plays very smoothly; the tension as you try desperately to dodge around the monsters to grab the falling crystals is intense. The only down-side I found was when I ran out of levels. I for one am definitely looking forward to the final release of this game, and if you've any sense you'll be keeping your eyes peeled for the hopefully-imminent review.
