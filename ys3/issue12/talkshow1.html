Terry: Yes, ladies and gentlemen, the most heart-breaking story I’m sure you’ve heard since yesterday’s show. The poor little mite was left all by himself at the tender age of fifteen. Now he’s forty, and we’ve got him on the show today. Please put your hands together for Bob!

(There is emotional applause as Bob shyly walks in from stage right, and plonks himself down in one of the easy chairs.)

Bob, welcome to the show.

Bob: Thanks for having me, Terry.

Terry: Now, Bob, you don’t know why we invited you here today, do you? You think you’re here as part of some feature on the growing pains of middle-age penguins.

Bob: Well, I am, aren’t I? That interview was all about that, right? This is Wildlife on One..?

Terry: Actually, the truth is bigger than that Bob. Today we’re going to be reuniting families, and we’ve managed to track down your two missing links: Nathan and Jon. (Everyone, especially Bob, is shocked and enthralled by this revelation.) In fact, we have them waiting in the wings right now. (The projector comes to life once more to show Nathan and Jon standing around, unkempt and with cigarettes in their mouths.) Nathan and Jon, would you please come in! (The shock on Bob’s face is marked as the two enter from stage left. They are even more surprised to see him, and Jon stops halfway across the stage, and bursts into floods of tears. Nathan is outraged.)

Terry: Welcome to the show, Na-

Nathan: What the hell is this? I thought I was here for a feature on the history of Speccy games, and you pull this? What the hell kind of a stunt is this?

Terry: Nathan, we’ve brought you here to reconcile you to Bob today. Twenty-five years ago you stepped out of his life without a word…

Nathan: Yeah?

Terry: … and we’re here to put your little family back together. Bob, is there anything you’d like to say?
