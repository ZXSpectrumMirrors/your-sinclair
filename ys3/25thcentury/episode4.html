In the year 2000, NASA launched another of America's cheap space probes. Aboard this compact-yet-desirable starship, editor Nathan 'Nat' Cross, having taken a wrong turn while trying to deliver the only disk with the twelfth issue of YS3 on it, was to experience cosmic forces beyond all comprehension.

In a freak mishap, his life support systems were frozen by temperatures beyond imagination.

YS3 was blown out of its planned trajectory into an orbit one thousand times more vast - an orbit which was to return Nat Cross to Earth what seemed like five hundred years later.

EPISODE FOUR: THE FUTURE'S RATHER DULL AND RAINY, ACTUALLY*
FADE TO: Space. Cold, dark space. (AAAAAAAARRRRGH! Jon) Ahem. Just kidding.

FADE TO: Doctor Hugh's office at Earth Space Fleet HQ. Nathan is gazing dreamy-eyed at the tightly-uniformed Colonel Willow. Colonel Willow punches him, and he falls over.

NAT: Ow.

WILLOW: I'm sorry, but you gave me no choice.

NAT: Oh, I don't mind. (He picks himself up.) Really, I don't mind.

HUGH: Now, look, that's enough of that chaps. Honestly. Now, Mr Cross, it seems that when you left Earth, you took with you the only copy of Issue Twelve of YS3.

NAT: Lawks, I'd forgotten about that. Well, I took everybody's work and my scraps of paper. Does that count?

HUGH: Mm, close enough. What we really want to know is - have you finished it?

NAT: No.

HUGH and WILLOW: WHAT?!

NAT: Only joshing. Of course I've finished it. You'd be rather annoyed at having read through almost four whole episodes of this nonsense only to discover that Issue Twelve still wasn't ready, wouldn't you?

HUGH: Rather, yes. So, where is it then?

NAT: It's in my spaceship.

WILLOW: The one that you ditched in the Atlantic?

NAT: That's the one. You did recover it, didn't you?

WILLOW: Oh... crivens.

HUGH: Well, colonel, it looks like you're going to have to go down for us. You'd best fetch your snorkel.
