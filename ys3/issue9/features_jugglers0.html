The Jugglers On
Nathan Cross

1. Why did you start YS3?

I got my first copy of Your Sinclair in 1987, and damned fine it was too. Sure, I was too young to appreciate some of the jokes, but now I understand the clever irony at the heart of every one. I grew up with that magazine, and when it was finally closed down six years later, I, like many others, was heartbroken.

Another six years passed, and I'd finally got onto the internet, and discovered the joys of comp.sys.sinclair. I looked long and hard for something like the Great Magazine, but the closest thing I could find was the digitally remastered YS2, and there were only three issues of that (and I'd read them six years earlier, when they were released in their original form on the YS covertape). Another month or so of relentless searching revealed that, indeed, there never had been a follow-up to that. I'd been toying with the idea of a Speccy-related website for nearly a year, and had put together some reasonable enough articles for it, but it was sitting on a rarely-visited Geocities site. Then it finally hit me - YS... YS2... YS3?

2. What inspires you to write something every month (or thereabouts)?

The readers. Definitely the readers. Knowing that I'm taking part in something larger than myself, and entertaining all those lovely people who very occasionally send us some mail. Usually when the issue is late, to ask what the hell we're up to. Sometimes we get nice mail. We got loads last month, for instance, but I knackered my PC and lost it all. Tch, eh?

3. What's your favourite Speccy game?

Ooh, there are so many! Like Jon, I enjoy beating someone to death with a mish-mash of fantastic monsters in Chaos, but I also quite like Target; Renegade. Ant Attack is bloody marvellous, too, and the amount of time I've spent playing Skooldaze is quite unbelievable.

4. Favourite snack?

Maryland Choc Chip cookies are my primary food group, and Coca-Cola provides me with all the sugar and caffeine I need to keep this blimmin' magazine going.

5. Favourite TV show?

Babylon 5 is without a doubt the greatest TV series the world has ever seen. Since that's not on any more, though, I'd have to say Buffy the Vampire Slayer. It's got cute girls, quality acting, superb fight scenes, and some really great writing to bind it all together. It's about the only thing on TV to make me cry this year (er, because I felt bad for the characters, okay?).

6. Favourite film?

Blade Runner is a truly amazing film, a sci-fi classic with an enduring film noir style. Oft imitated, never bettered. The Matrix is also a great movie; I don't care how many people think of it as nothing more than effects-laden kiddie-fodder, there's a serious and compelling core to the story that carries the whole film with effortless grace. And its basic premise makes you wonder - how do we know that what we see as reality is real?

There is no spoon.
