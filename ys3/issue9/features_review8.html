Issue Eight
May '00
...Until the eighth issue was three weeks late, that is. Keeping the layout of the previous issue, and staying away from the hideous optical nightmare that was issue six's colour scheme, last month's YS3 was more than a tad beefier on the features front, but once again all the reviews were written by Nathan, who by this time had written all of the reviews since issue six (except the Deviants one by Jamie, who'd written it a month earlier).
Supposedly released according to last month’s Next Month: April 15th 2000
Actually Released: May 12th 2000
OPI: 1250
Reviews: Jumping Jack’s Back 65°; Styx PC 55°; Turbo Lode 45°
Futureshocks: Fred, The Pyramid, Zub
Features: Jon derided the Playstation 2, new bug David defended the use of emulation, Actual Reality put Tapper through its paces, and Burgertime got back to basics with a trip to Starbucks. Do these guys ever stop?
Trainspotters Throughout History: James Bond
Where Are They Now? Ronald Rat
TAP included: Ahem.
What we really want to know is… if wishes were horses, what would hay be?
Random stuff in the small print: The YS3 team were unanimous in announcing Buffy the Vampire Slayer to be their TV highlights this month.
It was a special issue because: Jon only had to write a couple of the letters since so many came in from the readers! Woohoo! Now he just needs to get over the whole multiple-personality thing...
The Top 101 Things

Jon:

I can’t believe we’ve almost written 101 of the things! Special thanks go to my philosophy tutor for help last month, and to a random program my mum was watching on TV for help the month before. And now I can start recycling them! ...Er, is this thing still on? (Click.)

Nathan:

Jon's responsible for most of these things. In total I've written maybe fifteen. Despite this, whenever we go through the back issues, Jon can never remember which ones are his. He gradually gets increasingly confused, accusing me of writing the more bizarre ones and putting them in when he's not looking. So if we did recycle them, Jon would never know either. In fact, we have been recycling them since issue four.


Actual Reality

Jon:

Easily the most fun I’ve had in real life since I started the Cold War in the sixties. Guns, guts and glory... yep, this is my favourite part of the magazine and I’m really looking forward to “doing” Afterburner, Chase HQ and Barbarian... not to mention Advanced Lawn Mower Simulator.

Nathan:

I'm not so sure about this one. I mean, it started well, but lately things have just been getting silly. And do you know how much tin-foil costs? Do you? It's reached the stage where we rent RoboJon out for children's parties to make ends meet. Future of journalism, tchah.
