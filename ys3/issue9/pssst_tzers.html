css/csc flame war III

The annual flame war between comp.sys.sinclair and comp.sys.cbm has begun quite unceremoniously this year, with a test message forwarded from csc to css initiating The Starglider's own form of Mutually Assured Destruction - a crosspost asking 'Why was the C64 ever invented?'.

At present the flamewar itself has more or less died out, the last vestiges of resistance from csc being killed by scientific proof that the Spectrum's tapes load faster than the C64's disks.

+3e Launch

Garry Lancaster has announced an extended version of the last Spectrum to be built, the Plus Three. Capable of handling IDE disks, and with an improved DOS, you can find out more at http://www.spectrumplus3e.redhotant.com/

ZX Spectrum SE/Chloe

When not working on new Spectrum and Gameboy software, Andrew Owen likes to debug ROMs. A few ideas for standardised extensions later, and he comes up with the idea of the ZX Spectrum SE. Codenamed Chloe (after ZeDeX82's new daughter, see above), you can get more details from http://www.brandnewco.org/se/

ZXX

Another set of standards for extensions, that may possibly be absorbed into/supported by other projects, is ZXX. Again, you can check out the details at http://www.fox10050.freeserve.co.uk/ZXX.html

New General Emulation Site

http://members.fortunecity.com/computeremulation

Maintained by BAchus, a surprisingly pleasant chap who didn't throw a wobbly when asked not to post in HTML on Usenet, but instead apologised and took people's advice.

Unlike most emulation sites, which tend to cover all those horrible consoles and things, ComputerEmulation covers, well, just the computers. Although the Spectrum section is currently small, there are plans to expand it, and there's some good coverage of other formats, such as the BBC and C64. Go and have a look, you might well like it.
