Issue Six
February '00
And suddenly it all went horribly wrong. While it may appear to anyone who has seen the sixth issue that Nathan was suffering from colour- blindness, it did look absolutely stunning when it was posted. No, really. Oh, alright then, it didn't, it looked atrocious, but he posted it up anyway (the scamp). For those who braved the front page, things weren't too bad inside - in fact they were even better than the previous issue. Someone even submitted a game for the Smash .TAP - Chris Young's Journey to Yatton. Which, er, had a nice loading screen.
Supposedly released according to last month’s Next Month: January 20th 2000
Actually Released: February 8th 2000
OPI: 950
Reviews: Burps 70°; Deviants 51°; Jumping Jack II 90°
Futureshocks: Sabre Wulf PC, Sonic the Hedgehog, Willytron
Features: Burgertime braved the 929 from Bristol to Taunton in quite a random departure from anything that its name might have previously implied, awarding it 6/10. Actual Reality made its debut with Jon taking over the unenvious role of miner/millionaire Willy in Jet Set Willy, and Ernie the Psychotic Madman did his psychotic thing.
Trainspotters Throughout History: Harold from Neighbours.
Where Are They Now? Airwolf.
TAP included: Journey to Yatton by Unsatisfactory Software.
What we really want to know is... if the Terminator ever got built, how could it go back in time to stop itself being built?
Random stuff in the small print: What did you get for Christmas? Nathan "inflatable chair" Cross, Jon "the last Due South video I didn’t have" Hyde, Jamie "socks" Percival, Chris "a screaming headache" Young.
It was a special issue because: Of that colour scheme! What a sight for anyone to behold; even little Jimmy, Nathan’s colour-blind cousin of 5, fell to the floor screaming in agony upon seeing the contents page. Poor little tyke still has shakes whenever he goes near a monitor, which could disadvantage him for life.
Design and Redesign

Nathan:

For the first four issues the mag was laid out quite badly. Abominably so, in fact. Ish five (the Christmas ish) was alright, but I decided that ish six should be as bright and colourful as the original YS was. Unfortunately I misjudged things slightly, and we ended up with the most hideous background of all time ever.

Jon:

I was dead impressed with the YS3 logo right from the start, and the Scooby Doo pictures. The Christmas issue was kind of groovy, being all psychedelic . Closest I’ve ever come to an LSD trip... and convinced me to steer clear of drugs. WINNERS DON’T TAKE DRUGS (But pop stars, footballers and government ministers do... Ed). But if you want to see something REALLY colourful in its layout, more so that YS3, then try... er... Um... hmm. No, YS3 has the proverbial bull by the proverbial horns (Oo-er. Ed).
