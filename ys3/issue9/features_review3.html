Issue Three
August '99
By this time the team were getting into the swing of things, and had finished with the design of the magazine to get on with the content. This month saw the first Smash .TAP; Mr Sweeney's Hack Attack II (which should have been on the covertape of the last YS) and Professional Sheepdog Simulator, by Coran Sanths.

And then it went away, for a very long time indeed...
Supposedly released according to last month’s Next Month: August 26th 1999
Actually Released: August 26th 1999
OPI: 1347
Reviews: Chuckie Egg 72°; Basket Master 89°; Klass of '99 95°; Escape from the Planet of the Robot Monsters 77°; Rick Dangerous II 89°; Superkid 72°
Features: Ocean Colour Scenes, Jon's look at the wide world of Ocean film licences, focusing on films that he had actually seen.
Trainspotters Throughout History: Number 3 - John Major. Who'd have thunk it?
Where Are They Now? Captain Birdseye. And he made his comeback around this time…
TAP included: Hack Attack II by Gerard Sweeney, our first TAP. Gosh, brings a tear to my eye.
What we really want to know is... why do GAP want everyone in cords?
Random stuff in the small print: Clubbed to death by Rob D.
It was a special issue because: We actually had some news in this issue, along with the first review of a re-make. And Jon actually gave out some decent scores in his games. Will wonders never cease?
