Pssst! John Dow, 1999
I never played the original Pssst!. Blasphemy, I know, what with it being one of Ultimate's classics, but I just never seemed to get around to it. It's been sitting on a tape somewhere for the last few years, just gathering dust. It wasn't until I encountered the Rare website's retro section that I finally decided to play Pssst! properly.

And then along came this little bundle of binary data, courtesy of John Dow. (You know, given the number of games John produces, it's hard to believe that it's all the work of one man. I think he's actually got a team of clones working in his basement, constantly churning out code, working in shifts throughout the day while the real John Dow goes about the difficult business of nelefa frottling. Probably.) It's remarkably close to the original, Andy Noble's graphics appearing to be recoloured versions of the originals. In terms of gameplay it's perhaps a little easier, but that's no bad thing.
For the last few days I've been playing the two versions of Pssst! alternately, comparing the Ultimate classic with the remake. John Dow's version is a touch faster than the original, and seemingly easier too. This is due mainly to the enemies being a lot slower than in the original, giving you a great deal more leeway in dealing with them than Ultimate granted. This loosening-up of the game is a very good thing; it reduces the frustration factor that kicked in early on in the original. It's easier to begin with, and that pulls you in where the original didn't bother to. And a fair difficulty curve is always a good thing, since I'm notoriously bad at computer games. I wasn't always, you know. I used to be quite good, but then I got a Megadrive and things just went downhill from there. Still, I can play a mean game of Bomberman.

But I digress. The sound is pretty good; sound effects are simple, and there's a reasonable-enough little ditty playing in the background. It's quite a laugh, hammering about the screen, blasting the worms and wasps and spikey things that attempt to get close to your plant. The evil keyboard layout of the original is fixed here, too; Q-A-O-P-Space is the control mechanism here.
Given all of this wonderfulness, it seems a bit petty to dwell on the bad bits too long, so I'll run through them all quickly now... The title screen says 'Q to quit' but it isn't; there's no joystick/pad support; there's no instant quit key. Right, that's it. All the bad bits out of the way, and none of them tear-your-hair-out atrocities. In fact, John Dow's version of Pssst! is actually more fun than the original. It's a fairer game, it's easier to get into, and it looks and sounds better. Hurrah for John Dow's clones!
Graphics: 75° Coloured versions of the originals; sharp and cartoony.
Sound: 70° A simple background tune, and okay sound effects.
Life Expectancy: 75° Kill the bugs! Kill them all!
Gameplay: 70° It's a shoot-'em-up, but with a difference.

Summary: An attractive little blaster with a few neat twists, better than the Ultimate original it's based on.

Nathan Cross
