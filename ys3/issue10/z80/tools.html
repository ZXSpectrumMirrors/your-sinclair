A Jedi must know his tools. Look after them, he must. Understand them. Be one with your weapons, you must, if a Jedi you wish to be. Many different styles of Jedi, there are, and choose one you most prefer you should. Here, show you mine, I will. Teach you this style, Cor-An will, as I taught him.

Need an assembler, you will. Most important. Many fine assemblers there are, but stick to one we will. Assembly Studio 86, by Jeremy Goetsch, is meant for TI-86 programming. However, a Z80 processor the TI-86 uses, and so AS86 perfect for us it is. For Windows only, yes, but contains also a useful sprite editor it does.

Now, object code you will be able to generate. Need to turn it into a .TAP, you will. For this task use Lee Tonks' BINTAP, you should. Need a PC again, but problem this still should not be.

Set up batch file, I find fastest;

bintap myfile.obj myfile.tap specfile 30000

Armed well, you are now. Learn your weapons, one with them become. Important this is, or consign you to oblivion your destiny will.
